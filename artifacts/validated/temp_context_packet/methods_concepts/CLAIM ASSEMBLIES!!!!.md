## **The missing concept: Claim Assemblies**

A *Claim Assembly* is not a new schema.  
It’s a **temporary bundle** that answers one question:

*“What do we know, and what proves it?”*

You don’t change the artifacts.  
You **point to them together**.

---

## **The simplest way to operationalize what you already have**

### **STEP 1 — Define ONE claim (neutral, factual)**

Example claim (no intent, no blame):

**“Participation with DHS programs creates binding obligations that are not visible at the time of engagement.”**

That’s it. One sentence.

---

### **STEP 2 — Pull only supporting artifacts by role**

For that one claim, pull artifacts by **function**, not type.

#### **a) Authority anchors (what allows it)**

* 1–2 `authority_reference` artifacts

#### **b) Evidence anchors (what states it)**

* 1–2 `evidence_item` excerpts

#### **c) Reality anchors (what happens)**

* 1–2 `field_validation` artifacts

You’re not summarizing them.  
You’re **juxtaposing** them.

---

### **STEP 3 — Lay them out as a Claim Table**

This is the key move.

CLAIM: \[one sentence\]

Authority (allows it):  
\- AR-AOC-\[X\]  
\- AR-DHS-\[Y\]

Evidence (states it):  
\- EVID-\[A\]  
\- EVID-\[B\]

Field Validation (shows it):  
\- FV-\[C\]  
\- FV-\[D\]

No commentary.

Anyone reading this can:

* see the logic  
* check the sources  
* understand the conclusion *without you saying it*
